Okinawa Island

The castle pictured above is Shuri Castle, which is a castle in Okinawa known for its blend of Chinese and Japanese architectural influences. "On 31 October 2019, the main courtyard structures of Shurijo were destroyed in a fire" - Wikipedia

"It is the smallest and least populated of the five main islands of Japan.[5] The island is approximately 106 kilometres (66 mi) long, an average 11 kilometres (7 mi) wide,[6] and has an area of 1,206.98 square kilometers (466.02 sq mi)." - Wikipedia

"Among the prefectures of Japan, Okinawa has the youngest and fastest-growing population but has the lowest employment rate and average income. The island economy is primarily driven by tourism and the U.S. military presence, with efforts in recent years to diversify into other sectors" - Wikipedia
